# Conversion Brief — Terms of Service

> Procurement and legal-counsel trust page. The visible terms govern the brocode.ai website relationship; the strategic conversion is to give procurement a same-day path to Brocode's MSA template and commercial-terms summary.

## 1. Page Slug & URL
`/terms-of-service`

## 2. Primary Audience Persona
Senior Procurement Manager or General Counsel at a UAE federal entity, GCC tier-1 bank, regional insurer, or large enterprise (1,000–25,000 employees). They have been forwarded Brocode's name as a likely tenderer or as a sole-source AI consultancy. Before issuing an RFP response invitation, signing an NDA, or progressing a master services agreement, they need to scan the public-facing terms and (more importantly) get hold of Brocode's standard MSA, commercial-terms summary, and a clear position on liability caps, IP ownership, and warranty. They are not technical; they read commercial contracts every day and they spot boilerplate within 30 seconds.

## 3. Visitor Intent at Arrival
The visitor arrived via a footer link after browsing one or more service pages, a direct typed URL from a procurement checklist, or a Google search for `[Brocode MSA]` or `[Brocode terms of service]`. They are in early-to-mid **decision** stage on a vendor engagement and want two things in under 10 minutes: confidence that the visible website terms are professionally drafted (not template boilerplate), and a path to the standard contracting documents that will accelerate the MSA negotiation by 2–4 weeks.

## 7. The One Painful Problem
Procurement has 90 days to close an AI services contract and the legal team is already overcommitted on three other vendor MSAs — most AI vendors take 6–10 weeks to surface a usable MSA, liability position, and commercial-terms summary, which makes Brocode's contracting posture the rate-limiting factor in the deal.

## 8. The Stakes
- A slow contracting cycle pushes the project's go-live past the financial year, with consequent budget rebaselining and steering-committee escalation.
- An unclear IP position on model artefacts and training data is a deal-breaker for regulated customers and surfaces only after weeks of redline.
- A liability cap mismatch (1× fees vs. super-cap for IP, confidentiality, data protection) creates a procurement standoff that requires general counsel sign-off on both sides.

## 9. The Promise (H1 Direction)
Plain-English website terms plus a same-day MSA template and a one-page commercial-terms summary covering liability, IP, warranty, SLA, and termination — so procurement can pre-clear Brocode against internal contracting standards before the first call.

## 10. The Unique Mechanism
- **Two-document set:** publicly visible Website Terms of Service governing brocode.ai, plus an NDA-gated **Master Services Agreement** template (28 pages, English law / DIFC law variants) covering professional services, managed services, and software-licensed deliverables.
- **One-page Commercial Terms Summary** — a single landscape PDF covering liability cap (1× fees default, 3× super-cap for data protection, confidentiality, and IP indemnity), warranty (90 days re-performance), termination (for convenience with 60 days' notice; for cause with 30 days' cure), SLA (99.5 % monthly uptime for managed services, business-hours support default), and IP ownership (customer owns deliverables; Brocode retains pre-existing IP and a non-exclusive licence to general know-how).
- **Statement of Work template** with pre-filled commercial assumptions, milestone payment schedule, acceptance criteria framework, and change-control clause.
- **Acceptable Use Policy** governing brocode.ai usage and any hosted Brocode services.
- **Service Level Agreement** schedule with tiered support hours, response and resolution SLAs, and service-credit mechanism.

## 11. Top 3 Objections
- **O1:** "What is your standard liability cap and do you super-cap for data protection, IP indemnity, and confidentiality? We will not progress without that detail upfront."
- **O2:** "Who owns the model, the training data, and the fine-tuned weights? We require full ownership of deliverables and a clear position on residual rights to general know-how."
- **O3:** "What is your warranty position, your acceptance regime, and your termination-for-convenience window? We need to know the exit cost before we sign."

## 12. Proof Strategy
- The one-page Commercial Terms Summary, downloadable pre-NDA, with the headline numbers visible so procurement can pre-clear internally.
- The MSA template (NDA-gated) with a track-changes history showing it has been signed without redline by 12 of the last 18 customers — a strong signal that the starting position is market-acceptable.
- A "drafted and maintained by [named external counsel firm]" credit line.
- A side-by-side comparison of the two governing-law variants (DIFC law / English law) so the visitor knows both options exist.
- Named General Counsel contact with photograph, qualifications (LLB, LLM, admitted in [jurisdiction]), and a 45-minute Microsoft Teams booking link for an MSA pre-read call.
- Three anonymised procurement testimonials with the headline metric "MSA executed in X working days" (typical: 12–18 days from NDA to signature).

## 18. Section-by-Section Outline
1. **Hero & metadata** — typographic H1, version, last updated, governing law default, link to the Commercial Terms Summary download card.
2. **What this page is, and what it isn't** — a clear statement that the page covers the visible Website Terms of Service governing brocode.ai, and that engagement-level terms are governed by a separate signed MSA, available on request.
3. **Acceptance and scope** — who these terms bind, geographic scope, age and authority representations.
4. **Use of the website** — permitted use, prohibited use, automated access, scraping, AI training (explicit prohibition on scraping content for third-party model training without a written licence).
5. **Intellectual property** — brocode.ai content ownership, trademark, third-party marks, user-submitted content licence.
6. **Account and authenticated areas** — account creation, credential security, suspension and termination triggers.
7. **Disclaimers** — site provided as-is, no professional advice, third-party links.
8. **Limitation of liability (website)** — caps and exclusions specific to website use, distinguished clearly from the MSA-level caps.
9. **Indemnity** — user indemnity for misuse, reciprocal where Brocode is at fault.
10. **Governing law and jurisdiction** — DIFC Courts default, English-law variant where contracted.
11. **Changes to these terms** — versioning, changelog, notification mechanism.
12. **Commercial contracting with Brocode (MSA gateway)** — a clear, well-designed section explaining the MSA + Commercial Terms Summary + SoW + SLA + AUP document set, with the lead-magnet request form and the headline commercial terms visible (liability, IP, warranty, termination).
13. **General Counsel contact** — named GC, credentials, direct email, booking link.
14. **Changelog** — last six versions with date and substantive change summary.

## 20. Differentiation Angle vs Competitors
- **vs Big-4 consultancies (Accenture, Deloitte, PwC, EY)** — emphasise that the MSA and Commercial Terms Summary are available same-day under NDA; Big-4 procurement cycles for AI engagements typically start with a multi-week regional-legal review before a contract draft is shared.
- **vs hyperscaler professional services** — emphasise that Brocode's MSA is a single-document contract with one liability cap and one IP position, versus hyperscaler engagements which require stacking customer agreement + enterprise agreement + statement of work, each with separate caps and clauses.
- **vs offshore AI vendors** — emphasise DIFC law / English law options and a UAE-resident counterparty, versus offshore vendors that default to Indian or Singapore law and require enforcement abroad in the event of dispute.
- **vs boutique AI consultancies without a published contracting posture** — emphasise the published Commercial Terms Summary and the "signed without redline 12 of 18" track record, versus boutiques that negotiate every term from scratch.
