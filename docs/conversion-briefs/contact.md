# Conversion Brief — Contact

---

## 1. Page Slug & URL
`/contact`

## 2. Primary Audience Persona
A visitor who has already decided to reach out — either a buyer at the final stage of vendor selection, a procurement reviewer with a specific due-diligence question, a press or analyst contact, a partner firm seeking a co-sell conversation, a candidate routing around the careers form, or an existing client routing a support escalation. Five sub-personas served by the same page, each routed to a different team. The dominant sub-persona by volume is still the buyer (CIO, CDO or Head of Data at a UAE enterprise), but the page must not lose the other four to a single generic form.

## 3. Visitor Intent at Arrival
The visitor has typed "brocode contact" / "brocode dubai office" / "brocode email" directly, or has clicked the contact link from the footer or top nav of any other page on the site. **Decision** stage on the buying cycle, or operational on the other four. They want one of three things in this order: a direct human contact for their specific need, a confirmation that Brocode is responsive, and a low-friction path to start the conversation. They will not tolerate a generic "info@" inbox or a 12-field form. They will leave if the page does not route them within 30 seconds.

## 7. The One Painful Problem
The visitor is ready to start the conversation and cannot find the right human — most enterprise-AI vendor contact pages dump every inquiry into a single generic form that routes to a sales-development representative, costs the visitor 24–72 hours of triage delay, and signals an unserious operating posture.

## 8. The Stakes
- For a buyer at decision stage, a slow or mis-routed first contact is the most common reason a vendor drops off a shortlist — the buyer concludes "if this is how they answer the first email, the engagement will be worse."
- For a partner or co-sell inquiry, a contact page that doesn't surface a partnerships team signals that Brocode is not channel-ready, and the partner emails the next firm on their list.
- For a press or analyst contact at a publication deadline, the wrong inbox means the story runs without the Brocode quote.
- For an existing client with an escalation, a missing support route is a contractual breach risk and a churn signal.

## 9. The Promise (H1 Direction)
Direct lines to the actual humans who handle sales, partnerships, support, press and careers — each inquiry routed to a named team in under five minutes during UAE working hours, with explicit out-of-hours response commitments and a no-generic-inbox policy.

## 10. The Unique Mechanism
- **Triage by inquiry type**: a five-card top-of-page selector — sales / partnerships / support / press & analyst / careers — each routing to a different named contact with a different SLA.
- **Named human contacts, not role inboxes**: every inquiry type lists a real person, their photo, role, direct email and (where appropriate) direct WhatsApp.
- **Two physical offices, mapped**: Dubai (DIFC-adjacent) and Abu Dhabi (ADGM-adjacent), with Google Maps links, in-office working hours, and a visitor-coordinator contact for badge-in.
- **Explicit response SLAs**: sales — within 4 working hours; partnerships — within 1 working day; support — within 30 minutes during business hours, escalation pager out-of-hours; press — within 2 working hours during news cycles, immediate for deadline pieces; careers — within 5 working days.
- **A dedicated WhatsApp number per triage type**: not one generic Brocode WhatsApp, but five — each pre-filled with the relevant message template (see field 15).
- **A calendar self-book option for sales**: any qualified visitor can self-book a 30-minute call with a named principal directly, without an SDR layer.

## 11. Top 3 Objections
- **O1:** "Will my inquiry get routed to an SDR who knows nothing about the technical question I'm about to ask?"
- **O2:** "What are your actual response times — and do you respond during the weekend if we are on a procurement deadline?"
- **O3:** "Where are you actually located, and can I walk into your office for a face-to-face meeting?"

## 12. Proof Strategy
- Five named human contacts with photo, role and direct email — published on the page, not behind a form.
- Explicit, written response SLAs per inquiry type, with a published dashboard or quarterly transparency stat ("median sales response time: 1h 42m last quarter").
- Two physical office cards with photo, address, Google Maps link, in-office hours, and a phone number that connects to a real human during business hours (not an IVR maze).
- A "respond from any time-zone" note: Brocode's leadership is contactable on WhatsApp 7 days a week within reason — with the caveat that operational support has formal out-of-hours pager coverage and sales does not.
- A small "recent response time" widget pulled from the helpdesk and the CRM — updated daily, not a vanity stat.
- A founder's WhatsApp direct line in the sales triage card (with the caveat: "this goes to a real founder; please don't abuse it").

## 13. The Lead Magnet
(The contact page does not have a download. The "magnet" is the calendar self-book and the WhatsApp direct line — both faster than a download.) However, for visitors who arrive at the page in research mode rather than decide-to-contact mode, a small footer offer:
**"The Brocode One-Pager"** — a 2-page PDF with capabilities, team size, certifications, partner badges, top three case studies and direct contact lines for every triage type. Designed for the buyer to forward internally inside their organisation. Headline figure: every Brocode service line on a single page, with named human contacts and current AED rate-card ranges.

## 14. Primary CTA Wording
**"Email [named human] directly"** on each triage card (primary, repeated five times — one per card) — secondary on the sales card: **"Book a 30-minute call with a principal"** — tertiary, fixed floating: **"WhatsApp us"** (with a triage selector before the chat opens).

(One per triage type — the page renders the correct pre-fill based on which WhatsApp button is tapped.)

- **Sales:** "Hi Brocode — I'm exploring an AI engagement for [organisation], primary area [document intelligence / NLP / chatbot / ML / deep learning / MLOps]. Could we set up a 30-minute introduction?"
- **Partnerships:** "Hi Brocode — I'm reaching out about a partnership / co-sell / reseller opportunity from [partner firm]. Could you route me to the partnerships team?"
- **Support:** "Hi Brocode — I'm an existing client at [organisation] and I need to raise a support question regarding [project / system]. The priority is [low / medium / high / urgent]."
- **Press & analyst:** "Hi Brocode — I'm a [journalist / analyst] at [publication / firm] working on a story about [topic]. My deadline is [date]. Could you put me in touch with your press contact?"
- **Careers:** "Hi Brocode — I'm interested in an engineering role and have a quick question before I apply. My background is [short summary]."

## 16. Quote Form Fields
(There is no single quote form on this page. Each triage type renders a different short form, tuned to the inquiry. The sales form fields below are the most-used.)

**Sales triage form (5 fields):**
1. **Primary area of interest** (multi-select: document intelligence / NLP / conversational AI / machine learning / deep learning / MLOps / self-hosted LLM / not yet defined)
2. **Organisation and your role** (free text)
3. **Engagement horizon** (single select: this month / next quarter / 6 months / exploratory)
4. **Preferred response channel** (single select: email / phone / WhatsApp / scheduled call)
5. **Brief context** (free text, 500 characters max)

**Partnerships triage form (3 fields):** partner type / co-sell or reseller or technology partnership / brief context.

**Support triage form (4 fields):** client name / project name / severity / brief description.

**Press triage form (3 fields):** publication / topic / deadline date.

**Careers triage form** — redirects to the careers page form (see careers brief, field 16).

## 20. Differentiation Angle vs Competitors
- **vs Big-4 consultancies** — the Big-4 contact form routes to a generic "client services" team and the visitor never sees a named human; Brocode publishes five named humans with photos and direct emails on the contact page itself.
- **vs offshore Indian / Pakistani dev shops with a Dubai sales front-end** — those firms route all inquiries through a Dubai-based business-development team; Brocode publishes the actual engineering and partnerships principals as the primary contacts, with a UAE phone line that connects to a UAE-resident human.
- **vs hyperscaler PSO** — the hyperscaler contact flow is gated by a global sales-routing engine; Brocode publishes a direct calendar self-book for a UAE-based principal.
- **vs niche regional boutiques** — boutiques typically publish a single "info@" inbox; Brocode publishes five triage types, each with a named human and a written SLA.

## Production notes
- The named-human contacts on the triage cards must be re-confirmed monthly; any role change must be reflected within 48 hours.
- The response-SLA widget must pull from a real source (CRM and helpdesk); if the median falls below the published SLA, the dashboard must be updated, not hidden.
- The office phone numbers must connect to a real human during stated working hours; an IVR maze breaks the entire promise of this page.
- WhatsApp templates must be verified per the WhatsApp Business API content rules before deployment, especially the careers and press templates.
- The "founder's WhatsApp" line is a deliberate trust-signal — its routing must be reviewed quarterly to ensure it is genuinely answered by a founder and not silently rerouted.
- The five inquiry-type forms must each enforce the correct backend routing; mis-routing a press inquiry to sales is a brand-damage incident.
