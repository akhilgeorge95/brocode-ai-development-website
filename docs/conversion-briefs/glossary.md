# Conversion Brief — AI Glossary

---

## 1. Page Slug & URL
`/insights/glossary`

## 2. Primary Audience Persona
A blended awareness-stage audience: (a) Senior business buyer — CFO, COO, Head of Operations, Head of Customer Experience, or board member at a UAE / GCC enterprise — needing to understand a single AI term ("what is RAG?", "what is fine-tuning?", "what is an agent?") before a vendor meeting they were just added to; (b) Junior practitioner — graduate engineer, junior data scientist, or analyst — getting onboarded onto an AI project and looking for a definition with regional context. The shared trait: both want the shortest accurate answer, written without jargon, with a one-line example they can use in their next conversation.

## 3. Visitor Intent at Arrival
The visitor just searched for a single term — "what is RAG", "vector database explained", "Arabic LLM meaning", "MLOps definition" — and wants a clean answer in 60 seconds. Stage: **early awareness, broad SEO capture**. They are unlikely to convert on a quote form on this visit, but they will subscribe to a newsletter, share the page in Slack or WhatsApp, and remember Brocode as the source the next time they hear the term.

## 7. The One Painful Problem
The visitor is about to walk into a meeting in 12 minutes where their CEO will use a term they do not understand, and the top Google result is a 2,400-word US blog that takes ten minutes to skim — they need the term defined in 60 seconds with a regionally-relevant example.

## 8. The Stakes
- For the business buyer: losing face in front of the CEO and being seen as "behind on AI"; in a market where AI literacy is now a board-level competency, that perception lingers.
- For the junior practitioner: looking unprepared on day one of an AI project, getting reassigned, and missing a career window.
- For both: making a decision (vote yes on a vendor, vote no on a budget) based on a half-understood term.

## 9. The Promise (H1 Direction)
Every AI term you'll hear in a UAE boardroom, defined in under 60 seconds — plain language, regional examples, with a one-line "why this matters to your business" anchor on each entry.

## 10. The Unique Mechanism
- **A curated 240-term glossary** spanning: foundation-model concepts (LLM, transformer, attention, fine-tuning, RAG, embedding, vector database, context window, agent, tool use), classical ML concepts (supervised, unsupervised, reinforcement, gradient descent, overfitting), MLOps concepts (training, inference, drift, observability, model registry), governance and regulatory terms (model risk, bias, fairness, audit trail, data residency, TDRA, CBUAE, FSRA), and Arabic-specific terms (Khaleeji dialect, MSA, Arabic NER, tashkeel, tatweel).
- **A consistent three-block answer format** on every term: (1) Plain definition (≤40 words). (2) UAE / GCC example (one sentence). (3) Why it matters to your business (one sentence). No Wikipedia walls of text.
- **Linked depth:** every term links to the practitioner guide (`/insights/guides`) or service page (`/services/...`) that goes deeper on that term, so the awareness-stage visitor has an obvious next step when they are ready.
- **Searchable, filterable, and alphabetically anchored:** type-ahead search, filter by category (foundation, classical ML, MLOps, governance, Arabic-specific), A–Z jump bar.
- **Bilingual entries** on Arabic-specific terms: definition is rendered in both English and Modern Standard Arabic, side-by-side.
- **Last reviewed date** on every term — quarterly review cycle owned by the head of Arabic NLP and the head of Delivery.

## 12. Proof Strategy
- The three-block answer format is the proof — visible immediately on the first term the visitor lands on.
- Term-page subtitle: "Reviewed by [named principal engineer], [last reviewed date]" — a real human accountable for accuracy.
- Visible inbound count: "Most-read this month: RAG, AI agent, fine-tuning, data residency" — social proof of accuracy at scale.
- Newsletter promise printed next to the subscribe form: "One short email per month with three new terms and one practitioner guide. Unsubscribe in one click."
- A small "Suggest a term" link visible on every page — invites community contribution and signals that the glossary is alive.

## Production notes
- Every term must show the named reviewer and last-reviewed date; an unreviewed glossary is worse than no glossary.
- Arabic translations on Arabic-specific terms must be reviewed by a native MSA editor before publish.
- The "most-read this month" widget is auto-generated from analytics — do not hardcode.
- The Pocket Guide PDF must be regenerated whenever any of its 60 source terms is materially updated.
