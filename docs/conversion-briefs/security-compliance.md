# Conversion Brief — Security & Compliance

> Vendor due-diligence page. Not a destination — a conversion enabler that lets a CISO clear Brocode through an internal vendor review and leave with a Vendor Security Pack in hand.

## 1. Page Slug & URL
`/security-compliance`

## 2. Primary Audience Persona
Chief Information Security Officer, Head of Information Security, or Senior Vendor Risk Manager at a UAE federal entity, GCC tier-1 bank, regional insurer, or ADNOC-tier enterprise (1,000–25,000 employees). They have been forwarded Brocode's name by a business sponsor (a CIO or Chief Data Officer) and have been asked to clear the vendor through an internal third-party risk management (TPRM) process before procurement can issue a purchase order. They typically own a CyberGRX, OneTrust, or ServiceNow VRM workflow with 180–240 control questions and a fixed two-week turnaround to either approve, conditionally approve, or block the vendor. They are not the buyer; they are the gatekeeper.

## 3. Visitor Intent at Arrival
The visitor arrived from one of three paths: (a) a direct link from the Brocode sales team during a vendor-onboarding email, (b) a typed URL after the business sponsor told them "go check brocode.ai/security-compliance," or (c) a Google search for `[Brocode SOC 2]` or `[Brocode ISO 27001]` while filling in a TPRM questionnaire. They are deep in the **decision** stage on behalf of the business buyer. They want to leave the page within 8–12 minutes either with the Vendor Security Pack downloaded or with a security review call booked, so they can either tick boxes or escalate.

## 7. The One Painful Problem
The CISO is being asked to clear an AI consultancy through a 200-question TPRM workflow inside two weeks, and most AI vendors return a slide deck instead of a SOC 2 Type II report, a current ISO 27001 SOA, a recent penetration test summary, and a sub-processor list — forcing the CISO to either block the procurement or sign off on incomplete evidence.

## 8. The Stakes
- A signed-off vendor that later causes a data incident sits on the CISO's permanent record with the audit committee and the regulator (TDRA / SAMA / QCB).
- A blocked vendor delays a business sponsor's board-committed AI programme by a full quarter, and the CISO becomes "the office of no" in the next steering meeting.
- A conditional approval with open findings drags out for months in remediation calls that the CISO's team does not have headcount for.

## 9. The Promise (H1 Direction)
A complete vendor due-diligence pack — SOC 2 Type II, ISO 27001, recent penetration test, DPA, sub-processor list, and UAE PDPL / TDRA evidence — available the same day under NDA, with a named security contact who will sit a 60-minute review call with your TPRM team.

## 10. The Unique Mechanism
- **SOC 2 Type II** report (audit period covering the most recent 12 months) issued by a Big-4 affiliated CPA firm, available under NDA.
- **ISO/IEC 27001:2022** certificate plus the current Statement of Applicability (114 controls mapped) and most-recent surveillance audit letter.
- **Annual third-party penetration test** by a CREST-accredited firm; redacted executive summary shareable pre-NDA, full report under NDA.
- **UAE PDPL** (Federal Decree-Law No. 45 of 2021) and **TDRA Information Assurance** control mapping, including data-residency attestations for G42 Cloud, Khazna, and AWS UAE North deployments.

- **Incident response posture:** 24/7 on-call, 1-hour acknowledgement SLA, 72-hour regulator notification path aligned to PDPL Article 9 and GDPR Article 33.
- **Vendor Security Pack** — a single zipped, NDA-gated bundle so the TPRM analyst gets everything in one download instead of chasing five emails.

## 11. Top 3 Objections
- **O1:** "Show me a current SOC 2 Type II report, not a SOC 2 Type I or a self-attestation — and confirm the audit window covers the last 12 months."

- **O3:** "What happens on day one of a breach? Show me your incident response runbook, your notification SLAs to us as a customer, and the name of the person who will pick up the phone."

## 20. Differentiation Angle vs Competitors
- **vs Big-4 consultancies (Accenture, Deloitte, PwC, EY)** — emphasise speed of evidence delivery (Vendor Security Pack same-day; Big-4 InfoSec packs typically route through legal and arrive in 3–6 weeks) and a single named security contact rather than a regional partner pool.
- **vs hyperscaler MSPs and resellers** — emphasise that Brocode's own certifications (not the underlying hyperscaler's) cover the engagement; resellers commonly point at AWS / Azure compliance pages, which do not cover the reseller's own access to customer data.
- **vs offshore Indian / Eastern European AI vendors** — emphasise UAE-specific PDPL, TDRA, and NESA evidence, customer-managed keys on UAE-resident infrastructure, and a sub-processor register with country-of-processing transparency.
- **vs boutique AI consultancies without formal certifications** — emphasise SOC 2 Type II + ISO 27001 + ISO 42001 trifecta, with current audit windows, rather than a marketing claim of "enterprise-grade security".
