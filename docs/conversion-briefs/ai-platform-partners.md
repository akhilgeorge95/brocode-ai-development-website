## 1. Page Slug & URL
`/technology/ai-platform-partners`

## 2. Primary Audience Persona
Senior technical buyer — typically a Head of AI, Chief Data & AI Officer, or Director of Emerging Technology — at a UAE / GCC bank, sovereign-wealth-fund portfolio company, federal entity, or regional telco, 1,500–30,000 employees. Reports to the CIO and is the named sponsor on the AI vendor framework that procurement is about to issue. Their pre-procurement checklist explicitly demands "named platform partnerships at Premier / Advanced / Solution Provider tier or above" as a vendor pre-qualification gate. They are not impressed by partner logos alone — they want partner-side contacts, partnership tier, partnership year, and named joint case studies.

## 3. Visitor Intent at Arrival
The visitor just searched for vendor partnership credentials so they can paste a defensible paragraph into the procurement vendor-qualification document due this week. Stage: **late evaluation, immediately pre-procurement**. They will copy our partnership statement into their internal memo, forward our page to their procurement team, and request a partnership-led architecture review where someone from NVIDIA, Microsoft, AWS or Databricks joins the call alongside our team.

## 7. The One Painful Problem
Procurement has set a 10-day clock on the vendor-qualification submission and the visitor needs to produce a single page that lists named platform partnerships, tier, year and joint references — without that page, the vendor is disqualified before the technical evaluation even starts.

## 8. The Stakes
- Disqualification at vendor-qualification stage means the AI programme slips two procurement cycles — eight months — and the visitor explains the slip to the board.
- The CIO has been told this vendor is the strongest technical fit; if procurement disqualifies on a paperwork gap, the visitor's credibility with the CIO erodes.
- Competitor vendors (offshore consultancies and Big-4) routinely fail this gate because their "partnerships" are reseller agreements, not engineering partnerships — but only if our page makes the distinction obvious.

## 9. The Promise (H1 Direction)
Every named partnership procurement asks for — at the tier they require — backed by partner-side contacts, joint case studies, and a partnership-led architecture review you can book in 48 hours.

## 11. Top 3 Objections
- **O1:** "Anyone can put a logo on a website — what tier are you actually at, and can I speak to your partner contact?"
- **O2:** "Show me a joint case study, not a press release. Has this partnership actually shipped something in the UAE?"
- **O3:** "We need partner co-sell or co-build, not just resell — does your partnership unlock funding, joint engineering, or escalation paths we cannot get on our own?"

## 12. Proof Strategy
- Partner-side contact names (role, region, partnership year) revealed after lead capture.
- Joint case study downloads — one per major partner, anonymised at the client level but explicit at the partner level (e.g., "NVIDIA + Brocode: DGX reference deployment for a UAE federal entity").
- Co-sell and co-build agreement summaries — what the partnership unlocks for the client (joint funding, joint engineering hours, escalation routes).
- Partner-validated reference architectures hosted on partner portals where applicable (e.g., AWS Partner Solutions Finder, Microsoft AppSource listing).
- A live "Partnership tier ledger" — a table of every partnership, tier, year joined, last renewal date, named partner-side contact role.
