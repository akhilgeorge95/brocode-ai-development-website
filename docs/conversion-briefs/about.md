## 1. Page Slug & URL
`/about`

## 2. Primary Audience Persona
A member of an enterprise procurement or vendor-due-diligence committee at a UAE bank, federal entity, telecom or energy major — typically a Procurement Director, Vendor Management lead, Chief Compliance Officer, or a Board-appointed third-party-risk reviewer. They have been forwarded the Brocode shortlist by the business sponsor (the CIO or CDO) and are now running their standard due-diligence checks: who owns the firm, who runs it, where is it incorporated, what's the financial standing, what certifications and what regulator history. They are not the buyer; they are the gate. Their job is to find a reason to red-flag the vendor. A clean About page closes the gate quietly; a thin one triggers an escalation.

## 3. Visitor Intent at Arrival
The visitor has been emailed the link by the business sponsor, or has typed "Brocode about" / "Brocode founders" / "Brocode UAE" directly. **Decision** stage on the buying cycle, but technically gating. They are checking five things specifically: incorporation and legal entity, founder identities and backgrounds, team size and seniority, certifications and audits, and named clients (or references they can verify). They will not fill in a form. They will scan the page in 3–6 minutes and either close the tab satisfied or paste questions into an email to the business sponsor.

## 7. The One Painful Problem
A vendor-due-diligence reviewer cannot find, on the candidate vendor's site, the basics they need to clear the gate — incorporation, audited certifications, leadership identity, conflict-of-interest disclosure — and the resulting back-and-forth with the business sponsor delays the procurement window by two to four weeks.

## 8. The Stakes
- The reviewer is measured on cycle time and on post-engagement vendor incidents. A vendor that triggers either is a career-relevant problem.
- The business sponsor is waiting; an opaque About page makes the reviewer the bottleneck and damages the relationship with the CIO or CDO.
- If the reviewer red-flags Brocode and a competitor wins, the original sponsor loses face — and the reviewer may be excluded from the next strategic-sourcing event.
- An incomplete or inconsistent disclosure (different registration numbers across pages, missing founder photos, no certificate scans) is, in itself, an audit observation in many of these institutions.

## 9. The Promise (H1 Direction)
A UAE-headquartered AI engineering firm, founded and led by named principals, audited to ISO 27001 and SOC 2 Type II, with offices in Dubai and Abu Dhabi, transparent ownership, and a published team — built for enterprises that need to know exactly who they are buying from.

## 10. The Unique Mechanism
- **Full transparency by design**: every founder is named with photo, full LinkedIn, prior employers, education, and the founding date. No anonymous principals, no PO-box-only address.
- **Audited compliance posture**: ISO 27001 (information security), SOC 2 Type II (controls), CSA STAR registry presence, ISO 9001 (quality), with downloadable certificate scans and the auditor's identity disclosed.
- **Regulatory bona fides**: registered legal entity in the UAE (Dubai mainland + ADGM presence), VAT-registered, signed CBUAE and DFSA-compliant DPAs available on request, financial standing letter from a Tier-1 UAE bank available on request.
- **Named operating team**: every engineer above mid-level is publicly listed with photo, role, country of residence (most UAE), and a public profile link. No "team of 50+ experts" claim without the actual list.
- **Regional footprint**: physical offices in Dubai (DIFC or Downtown adjacent) and Abu Dhabi (ADGM or Masdar adjacent), plus an engineering bench in Egypt and Pakistan for cost-tiered support work — fully disclosed, not hidden.

## 11. Top 3 Objections
- **O1:** "Who actually owns this firm, and is there any ultimate-beneficial-owner exposure we need to flag — sanctioned jurisdictions, PEPs, undisclosed parent companies?"
- **O2:** "Are you actually in the UAE, or is this a hyped offshore shop with a registered agent in Dubai?"
- **O3:** "What's your financial standing, and what's your continuity story if a key founder leaves — are we exposed to single-person risk?"

## 12. Proof Strategy
- A named-founder section with full LinkedIn links, photos, biographies, prior employers (with permission), and signed founders' statement.
- Downloadable ISO 27001 and SOC 2 Type II certificates as PDFs — with the auditor name and audit period visible.
- An ownership and corporate structure diagram, with the legal entity name, license number, jurisdiction of incorporation, and parent / sister entities disclosed.
- A team page link with photos, roles, residency and public profile links for every engineer above mid-level.
- Office photos (real, recent, with timestamps): Dubai and Abu Dhabi locations, with Google Maps links.
- A bank-issued financial standing letter available on request through the procurement contact (link in field 19).
- A named procurement contact (real person, real email, real direct line) for due-diligence questions — not a generic info@ inbox.

## 17. Hero Media Direction
A grounded, photographic hero — not animated, not abstract. A single high-resolution photograph of the Brocode Dubai office on a real working day, showing the engineering floor, named senior engineers visible at desks, the Brocode wordmark on the back wall, a glimpse of the city skyline through a window. Slight warm grade. Overlaid in the lower third, a single short H1 and three subtle fact badges: "Founded 2021 · Dubai & Abu Dhabi · ISO 27001 + SOC 2 Type II". No stock photography. No motion graphic. The procurement reviewer is buying trust, not motion.

## 18. Section-by-Section Outline
1. **Hero** — real office photograph with the founding-year, locations and certifications fact-badges.
2. **The founding story** — three short paragraphs: why Brocode was founded, what gap in the UAE market it was built to close, and the founders' commitment to UAE-resident engineering.
3. **Founders** — three to five founder cards: photo, name, role, biography (200–300 words each), public LinkedIn, prior employers, education.
4. **Senior leadership** — a second band of named senior engineers and operating leads beyond the founders.
5. **Team and engineering bench** — group photograph plus a link to the full team page; a transparent breakdown of where the engineering bench sits (UAE-resident vs Egypt vs Pakistan, by percentage).
6. **Offices and regional footprint** — Dubai and Abu Dhabi office photos with Google Maps links; the Egypt and Pakistan delivery bench locations disclosed.
7. **Corporate structure, licensing and certifications** — corporate-structure diagram, trade licence, VAT, ISO 27001 and SOC 2 Type II certificates available for download.
8. **Financial standing and continuity** — a paragraph on financial standing (bank reference available on request) and key-person continuity policy.
9. **Partnerships and memberships** — NVIDIA Inception, AWS Advanced Tier, G42 Cloud, Databricks, Snowflake, Hugging Face, plus chamber memberships (Dubai Chamber, Dubai Chamber of Digital Economy).
10. **Due-diligence pack CTA** — full-width band with the named procurement contact and the download form.
11. **FAQ for procurement** — UBO, sanctions exposure, sub-contractors, insurance, dispute resolution.
12. **Footer** — full sitemap, registration numbers, addresses, regulatory disclaimers.

## 20. Differentiation Angle vs Competitors
- **vs Big-4 consultancies** — comparable certification posture (ISO 27001, SOC 2 Type II), but with the founder and engineer identities published — Big-4 firms list partners but not the engineers a procurement reviewer is actually about to onboard.
- **vs offshore Indian / Pakistani dev shops** — Brocode is UAE-headquartered, audited locally, with a Dubai mainland licence and an ADGM presence; the offshore bench is disclosed (not hidden) and sits under UAE-signed DPAs that the offshore shops cannot themselves offer.
- **vs niche regional boutiques** — Brocode publishes a full team, full certifications and a downloadable due-diligence pack; many regional boutiques still rely on a single-founder website with no audit trail.
- **vs hyperscaler PSO (AWS ProServe, Azure CXP, Google PSO)** — Brocode is a UAE-resident counterparty with a UAE-signed contract and AED billing; the hyperscaler PSO contract sits with a US or Ireland entity and rarely passes the procurement reviewer's same-jurisdiction test without a layered reseller.

## Production notes
- All founder photographs and biographies must be re-confirmed with the founders quarterly; any LinkedIn-employment change must be reflected within 30 days.
- The certificate PDFs must be the auditor-issued originals (not designed marketing summaries); the auditor's name and audit period must be visible on the certificate.
- The named procurement contact must be a real person with a phone number — never a generic info@ inbox.
- Office photos must include a timestamp watermark; refresh annually at minimum.
- All regulatory references (CBUAE, DFSA, VARA, ADGM) must be reviewed by counsel before publish.
