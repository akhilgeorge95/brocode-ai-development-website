## 1. Page Slug & URL
`/careers`

## 2. Primary Audience Persona
Two overlapping candidate personas. **Persona A:** a senior ML / AI engineer (5–12 years' experience) currently working in London, Berlin, Toronto, Bangalore, Singapore or São Paulo at a FAANG, a tier-1 fintech, a research lab or a high-growth AI scale-up — considering a regional move to the UAE for tax, climate, family or proximity-to-applied-AI reasons. **Persona B:** a UAE-based ML / data / software engineer (3–8 years' experience) currently at a Big-4 advisory, a hyperscaler PSO team, a UAE bank's internal data team or an offshore shop's Dubai sales front-end — looking to upgrade to a deeper engineering culture with named work and on-premise GPU access. Both will scrutinise compensation, GPU access, project depth, and visa or relocation logistics within the first three minutes of a careers page.

## 3. Visitor Intent at Arrival
The visitor has searched "ml engineer jobs dubai", "ai engineer uae salary", "senior data scientist abu dhabi", or arrived via a LinkedIn job post, an engineering-team-member's referral, an AI Twitter thread, or a Hugging Face / GitHub recruiter touch. **Evaluation** stage for their next career move — typically actively interviewing at two to four firms. They want to triage Brocode into "apply", "watch", or "no" inside one session. The conversion goal is a strong, qualified application — not volume.

## 6. Geographic Targeting
- **Primary:** UAE — Dubai and Abu Dhabi (in-country candidates and active relocators)
- **Secondary:** India (Bangalore, Hyderabad, Pune), Pakistan (Lahore, Karachi, Islamabad), Egypt (Cairo, Alexandria) — strong regional engineering corridors with established UAE relocation pathways
- **Tertiary:** UK (London), Germany (Berlin), Canada (Toronto), Singapore, Brazil (São Paulo) — passive senior-engineer pools considering regional moves

## 7. The One Painful Problem
A senior ML engineer is looking for a role that combines applied depth (real GPU access, real production work, real publications and conference time), regional opportunity (UAE residency, tax-free salary, family infrastructure), and a credible engineering culture — and most UAE postings are either generic data-analyst roles at a bank, sales-engineer roles at a hyperscaler, or vague "AI consultant" roles at a Big-4.

## 8. The Stakes
- The candidate is at a 12–18 month career-move window; the wrong choice burns the window and forces another full job hunt.
- For relocating candidates, the move involves family, schools, visa and residency — the cost of a bad fit is six-figure and personal.
- For UAE-based candidates, the upgrade is to a role with named work and external visibility (publications, GitHub, conference talks) — the wrong move keeps them invisible for another two years.
- Compensation transparency matters: in the UAE market, opaque salary bands cost candidates 20–40 % at the offer stage, and a careers page that hides bands signals that pattern.

## 9. The Promise (H1 Direction)
A UAE-based engineering home for senior ML, deep-learning, NLP and MLOps engineers — named work, on-premise GPU access, published bands, full relocation support, and the kind of engineering culture that publishes papers, owns GitHub repositories, and ships production.

## 10. The Unique Mechanism
- **Named work, externally visible**: every engineer at Brocode above mid-level has a public profile on the team page, with publications, conference talks and GitHub repositories explicitly supported.
- **On-premise GPU and frontier compute access**: NVIDIA DGX clusters, H100 / H200 on G42 Cloud, Hugging Face Enterprise — engineers do not queue behind a corporate IT ticketing system.
- **Published compensation bands**: every role lists an AED salary band (tax-free), the standard benefits envelope, and the relocation package range — no negotiation games at the offer stage.
- **A formal Learning & Growth allowance**: AED 15,000 per engineer per year for conferences (NeurIPS, ICML, KDD, EMNLP), books, courses and certifications, plus four conference days off the engagement clock.
- **20 % time on research and open-source**: every engineer can spend one day a week on internal research, open-source contribution, paper writing, or a Hugging Face model card — with publication review by the head of research.
- **Full UAE relocation support**: employment visa, family visa sponsorship, three months of company-paid temporary accommodation, school-search assistance, shipping allowance, and a UAE banking and rental onboarding coordinator.
- **A two-stage interview that respects time**: a 45-minute technical screen, a paid 1-day take-home (compensated at the engineer's day rate), and a 1-day on-site with the actual team — no whiteboard hazing, no eight-round marathon.

## 11. Top 3 Objections
- **O1:** "How is this different from joining a Big-4 advisory in Dubai — am I going to end up on PowerPoint duty for a banking client?"
- **O2:** "Compensation transparency — what does a Senior ML Engineer actually take home in AED, and what's the relocation package look like with a spouse and two children?"
- **O3:** "What is the engineering culture really — code review, on-call, publication policy, open-source policy — and who is the technical leadership I'd be reporting to?"

## 12. Proof Strategy
- A named engineering-team page (linked prominently) with every senior engineer's photo, role, LinkedIn, GitHub, publications and conference talks.
- Published salary bands on every open role: AED-denominated, with the benefits envelope and relocation package range printed in the role page itself.
- A "day in the life" section with three engineer-written diary entries — what a real Tuesday looks like, including code review cadence, GPU access, meeting load.
- Public artifacts: links to Brocode-engineer Hugging Face model cards, GitHub repositories, NeurIPS / EMNLP / ICML attendance, blog posts authored by named engineers.
- A short video (90 seconds) from the head of engineering on culture, on-call and publication policy.
- A relocation FAQ section answering the spouse-visa, school-search, banking, rental, schooling and tax questions explicitly.
- Glassdoor and LinkedIn ratings disclosed (with link), and a written commitment to respond to every application within five working days.

## 13. The Lead Magnet
**"The UAE Senior ML Engineer Compensation & Relocation Guide 2026"** — 24-page PDF written for engineers, not recruiters. Covers AED tax-free salary bands across Brocode and the wider UAE market (Big-4, banks, hyperscalers, scale-ups), cost-of-living modelling for a family of four in Dubai vs Abu Dhabi, visa and residency mechanics, schooling and healthcare cost ranges, and a relocation timeline checklist. Headline figure: a Senior ML Engineer (L5-equivalent) in Brocode's published band takes home roughly **38–52 % more disposable income** than the equivalent FAANG band in London at current FX after rent and schooling, depending on family size.

## 20. Differentiation Angle vs Competitors
- **vs Big-4 advisories in the UAE (Accenture, Deloitte, KPMG, EY, IBM Consulting)** — Brocode publishes salary bands, ships code rather than slides, gives engineers external visibility (publications, GitHub, conference talks), and runs a two-stage interview rather than the Big-4 six-round process.
- **vs hyperscaler PSO teams (AWS ProServe, Azure CXP, Google PSO, regional offices)** — Brocode engineers work across the stack and across clouds rather than being locked to a single vendor's managed service; on-premise GPU access is built-in.
- **vs UAE bank internal data teams** — Brocode engineers work on multiple clients and domains across a year rather than a single internal backlog; project depth and publication freedom are explicit.
- **vs offshore Indian / Pakistani dev shops with a Dubai front-end** — Brocode hires the engineer onto the UAE entity directly, pays in AED into a UAE bank account, and offers full relocation support including family visa, rather than the body-shop rotation model.
- **vs scale-ups in London / Berlin / Toronto / Singapore** — published transparent comparison of AED tax-free disposable income vs the equivalent base salary in those cities after tax, rent and schooling; the compensation and relocation guide is the lead magnet for this comparison.

## Production notes
- Every open-role card must show an AED salary band, with the band reviewed and re-confirmed by the head of people at least quarterly.
- The five-working-day application response commitment must be tracked in the ATS, with a public dashboard or quarterly stat published.
- The compensation comparison figures (38–52 % more disposable income) must be re-validated annually with up-to-date FX, rent and school-fee data.
- All engineer photographs and diary entries require written consent and a refresh date; entries older than 12 months must be re-confirmed.
- The "day in the life" entries must be written by the engineers themselves, not by marketing; voice and authenticity are the conversion mechanism.
- All compensation, visa and relocation claims must be reviewed by counsel and the head of people before publish.
