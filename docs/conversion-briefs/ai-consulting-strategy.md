## 7. The One Painful Problem
The board has demanded "an AI strategy" and the CXO has neither the time nor the in-house bandwidth to convert 60+ candidate use-cases scattered across business units into a sequenced, costed, governable plan — and the Big-4 quote on the table is USD 4–6 million for a 16-week slide engagement with a junior-heavy team that does not build anything afterwards.

## 8. The Stakes
- The next board meeting is on the calendar. Walking in without **the plan** is a personal reputation event for the CXO sponsor.
- The competitive cohort (peer banks, peer telcos, peer sovereign holdings) is publicly making AI announcements. Being seen to be late carries strategic-positioning cost.
- The wrong roadmap — one that overweights generative AI demos and underweights data foundations, governance, and talent — burns 18–24 months and a seven-figure budget before the executive committee admits it.
- Inside the entity, business-unit heads are starting their own shadow AI initiatives. Without a credible central plan, the AI estate fragments before it is born.
- For regulated entities, the regulator (CBUAE, SAMA, DFSA, FSRA) is publishing AI-governance expectations on a quarterly cadence. The strategy must be regulator-defensible from day one.

## 9. The Promise (H1 Direction)
A board-grade AI strategy and 24-month roadmap — diagnostic, value pools, sequenced portfolio, operating model, governance, talent plan, capex envelope — delivered in 8 weeks by a named senior team, with a builder behind every page.

## 10. The Unique Mechanism
- The **"Brocode AI Strategy Sprint" — an 8-week, fixed-fee diagnostic & roadmap engagement** delivered by a partner-led pod (1 partner, 1 strategy director, 1 principal engineer, 1 data architect, 1 governance / regulatory lead) — every CV visible to the customer before contract signature. No junior pyramid.
- A proprietary **5-lens diagnostic framework**: **Value (use-case economics)**, **Data (foundations & lineage)**, **Tech (platform readiness)**, **People (skills & operating model)**, **Governance (regulator & risk)**. Each lens scored on a 1–5 maturity grid with named, observable evidence — not a survey.
- A **value-pool model** built bottom-up from the customer's own P&L lines and operational KPIs — not a McKinsey-style industry-average benchmark transplanted onto the customer. Each candidate use-case carries a **NPV / payback / risk** triple, and the portfolio is sequenced by dependency and by data-readiness.
- A **regulator-mapped governance design**: the roadmap deliverable explicitly maps each workstream against **CBUAE / SAMA / DFSA / FSRA / ADGM** AI-governance and model-risk expectations, plus **EU AI Act** high-risk articles where the entity operates in the EU.
- A **build-or-buy decision per use-case**, supported by Brocode's reference library of 60+ delivered builds — so the strategy already knows what is cheap to buy, what must be built, and what is not yet credible to attempt.
- A **"builder behind every page"** commitment: every roadmap workstream has a named Brocode engineer or architect attached, so the customer can move directly from strategy to delivery without re-procuring.
- Optional **on-site executive working group** model: weekly half-day with the CXO sponsor and the partner, for the full 8 weeks, on the customer's site — not Zoom.
