# Conversion Brief — Privacy Policy

> Legal page that doubles as a trust signal for data-protection reviewers. Must satisfy the legal floor while reading clearly enough that a DPO can use it as evidence of maturity.

---

## 1. Page Slug & URL
`/privacy-policy`

## 2. Primary Audience Persona
Two readers, equally weighted:
- **Reader A — Data Protection Officer or Legal Counsel** at a prospective customer (UAE / GCC / EU). They are reviewing Brocode's privacy practices as part of a Data Processing Agreement negotiation or a vendor onboarding workflow. They want to see whether the policy aligns with UAE PDPL (Federal Decree-Law No. 45 of 2021), KSA PDPL, GDPR (Regulation (EU) 2016/679), and the DIFC Data Protection Law No. 5 of 2020. They are checking lawful bases, retention windows, sub-processor transparency, international transfer mechanisms, and named DPO contact.
- **Reader B — end-user visitor** of brocode.ai (a prospect researching Brocode, a candidate, a journalist) who clicked the footer link and wants a plain-English summary of what Brocode does with their data.

## 3. Visitor Intent at Arrival
Reader A is in late **decision** stage on a DPA negotiation and needs the policy to be a credible cross-reference to the contract. Reader B is in **awareness** stage and just wants confidence in 60 seconds that the site is operated by a serious company. The page must satisfy both: an executive summary up top for Reader B, a properly structured legal text for Reader A, and a clear path to either request the DPA template or contact the DPO.

## 7. The One Painful Problem
A DPO has been handed a draft DPA from the procurement team and needs the corresponding privacy policy to confirm that lawful basis, retention, sub-processor handling, international transfer mechanism, and data-subject rights are described consistently — most vendor privacy pages are either a generic template that contradicts the DPA or a wall of unstructured text that takes 45 minutes to parse.

## 8. The Stakes
- An inconsistency between the DPA and the privacy policy is a finding in the DPO's review and either blocks the contract or forces a redline cycle.
- A vague international-transfer clause means the DPO has to escalate to in-house GDPR counsel, adding two weeks to the procurement timeline.
- For Reader B, a poorly written policy is a soft trust signal that the company does not take its data obligations seriously, and bounces them off the site.

## 9. The Promise (H1 Direction)
A privacy policy that reads as if it was written by the DPO who has to defend it — with a plain-English executive summary, a clear structure mapped to UAE PDPL, GDPR, and DIFC DP Law articles, named DPO contact, and a one-click path to request the DPA template.

## 10. The Unique Mechanism
- **Dual-layer policy:** a 60-second executive summary at the top followed by the full legally binding text.
- **Article cross-reference:** every section heading carries a small marginal cross-reference to the relevant article in UAE PDPL, KSA PDPL, GDPR, and DIFC DP Law.
- **Last-updated semantic versioning** (e.g., v3.2 — 14 May 2026) with a public changelog showing what changed between versions and why.
- **Named DPO** (full name, photo, email, postal address, ISO 27701 Lead Implementer credential) — not a generic `privacy@` mailbox.
- **DPA template available on request** — UAE PDPL + GDPR + DIFC DP Law aligned, with standard contractual clauses (EU SCCs 2021/914) and UAE PDPL appropriate-safeguards addendum pre-filled.
- **Data subject rights portal** for access, rectification, erasure, restriction, portability, and objection requests — with a 30-day SLA and an audit trail.

## 11. Top 3 Objections
- **O1:** "Your policy says you transfer data internationally. What's the lawful mechanism — SCCs, adequacy decision, or PDPL appropriate safeguards — and where is it documented?"
- **O2:** "Who is your DPO, what is their qualification, and how do I reach them outside this contact form?"
- **O3:** "How does your retention schedule actually work — is there a per-data-category retention period, or is it 'as long as necessary' boilerplate?"

## 12. Proof Strategy
- A retention schedule table with rows per data category (account data, billing data, support tickets, marketing data, candidate data, model training data, telemetry) and the specific retention window in months with the deletion mechanism named.
- Named DPO with full credentials block (ISO 27701 LI, CIPP/E, member of IAPP) and direct email + Microsoft Teams booking link.
- A short, dated changelog at the bottom showing the last six versions and the substantive change in each.
- A "verified by Big-4 affiliated legal counsel" line with the law firm's name (under appropriate disclosure).
- Cross-reference badges to the underlying laws: UAE PDPL, KSA PDPL, GDPR, DIFC DP Law, UK GDPR.

## 13. The Lead Magnet

Headline figure: **"The same DPA we have signed with 40+ regulated customers — pre-redline, ready for your in-house counsel."**

## 18. Section-by-Section Outline
1. **Hero & metadata** — typographic H1, version, last updated, next review date.
2. **60-second executive summary** — five-bullet plain-English summary of who Brocode is, what data is processed, who it is shared with, where it goes, and how to exercise rights.
3. **About this policy** — scope (brocode.ai, customer engagements where Brocode is controller, candidate processing), the laws it is built to satisfy, and the cross-reference key used in the margins.
4. **Who we are and how to contact us** — legal entity name, registered address (UAE), trade licence number, EU representative under GDPR Art. 27, UK representative under UK GDPR.
5. **The personal data we process** — categorised list: website visitors, prospective customers, customers and their authorised users, candidates, suppliers, model training data (with explicit note on customer data handling under DPA).
6. **Lawful bases** — table with each processing purpose and the lawful basis under UAE PDPL Article 4 and GDPR Article 6 (and Article 9 special categories where applicable).
7. **How we use your data** — operational purposes, marketing, analytics, model improvement, security monitoring.
8. **Who we share data with** — sub-processor list (link to register), professional advisers, law enforcement, corporate transactions.
9. **International transfers** — mechanism (SCCs 2021/914, UAE PDPL appropriate safeguards, adequacy decisions where applicable), destinations, and how to obtain a copy of the transfer impact assessment.
10. **Retention schedule** — per-category table with retention window and deletion mechanism.
11. **Your rights and how to exercise them** — access, rectification, erasure, restriction, portability, objection, withdrawal of consent; the data-subject rights portal; 30-day SLA; appeal path.
12. **Security and ISO 27001 / ISO 27701 controls** — short reference to the security page and the relevant attestations.
13. **Children's data** — Brocode does not knowingly process children's data; deletion path if discovered.
14. **Cookies and online tracking** — short summary and link to the full cookie policy.
15. **Changes to this policy** — versioning, changelog, notification mechanism.
16. **Contact the Data Protection Officer** — named DPO, credentials, email, postal address, Microsoft Teams booking link.
17. **Changelog** — last six versions with date and substantive change summary.

## 19. Internal Linking Strategy
1. `/security-compliance` — cross-link from sections 8 (sub-processors), 9 (transfers), and 12 (security); the Vendor Security Pack on that page satisfies the TOMs evidence question.
2. `/cookie-policy` — cross-link from section 14; the cookie policy is the operational extension of the privacy policy.
3. `/terms-of-service` — cross-link from section 4; the ToS and Privacy Policy together govern the brocode.ai relationship with end-users.
4. `/contact` — secondary contact path beyond the DPO, for general inquiries.
5. `/about` — links from section 4 (who we are) to the corporate "about" page.

## 20. Differentiation Angle vs Competitors
- **vs generic Termly / iubenda-generated policies on AI vendor sites** — emphasise that the policy is bespoke, cross-referenced to UAE PDPL article numbers, and reviewed by named external counsel; generic generated policies cannot match this depth and fail DPO scrutiny.
- **vs Big-4 consultancies** — emphasise a single, named DPO who picks up the phone, versus a generic `privacy@` mailbox routed to a regional shared services team.
- **vs hyperscaler-bundled policies** — emphasise that the policy covers Brocode as controller and processor across its own services, not just the underlying hyperscaler; many AI-vendor policies hand-wave the vendor's own processing and point at AWS or Azure.
- **vs offshore AI vendors** — emphasise UAE PDPL alignment with article-level cross-references, UAE legal entity, and a UAE-resident DPO; offshore vendors typically default to a GDPR-only policy with no UAE coverage.

## Production notes
- The policy must be reviewed by external counsel on a six-monthly cadence; the "next scheduled review" date in the hero must be auto-populated from that schedule.
- The changelog must be append-only and version-controlled; no silent edits.
- The DPO's name, credentials, and photograph must be approved by the DPO themselves before publish.
- The retention schedule must be cross-checked against the actual configured retention windows in production systems annually.
