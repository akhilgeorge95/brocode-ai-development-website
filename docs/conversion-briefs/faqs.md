## 7. The One Painful Problem
The visitor has every technical answer they need, but procurement, legal, and the board sponsor each have one or two unresolved commercial questions — and chasing those answers through three more vendor calls will slip the SoW into next quarter.

## 8. The Stakes
- A slipped SoW means the AI use case slips one budget cycle — three to six months — and the business sponsor loses interest.
- An unanswered IP-ownership or exit-clause question becomes a legal-team veto in the contract-review meeting.
- An unclear pricing model means the procurement team rejects the SoW for "insufficient cost certainty" and the visitor has to rerun the RFP.
- The wrong answer here — or no answer at all — pushes the visitor to a less-fit vendor whose FAQ page was clearer.

## 9. The Promise (H1 Direction)
The 40 commercial, legal, and operational questions every GCC enterprise asks before signing an AI consultancy SoW — answered in writing, with pricing bands, timeline norms, IP language, exit-clause templates, and a direct route to a quote when the answers fit.

## 10. The Unique Mechanism
- **40 curated FAQs organised in seven blocks:** Pricing & commercial model (6); Timeline & delivery (5); Scope & change control (4); IP, data, and model-weights ownership (6); Data residency & sovereignty (5); Security, audit, and regulator alignment (6); Hand-over, run, and exit (8).
- **Direct answers, not deflections:** every answer is between 80 and 220 words and includes a pricing band, a timeline norm, a contract-language sample, or a regulator-reference where appropriate.
- **Sample answer specifics:**
  - "How much does an AI project cost?" — published bands by engagement type: Discovery (4 weeks): USD 35K–80K; Build (12–26 weeks): USD 180K–950K; Run (annual): USD 60K–340K — calibrated to UAE / GCC market.
  - "Who owns the model weights and the code?" — default contract clause: client owns model weights, fine-tuned adapters, and all engagement-generated code; Brocode retains the methodology and the reference architectures. Joint background-IP register attached to every SoW.
  - "What happens when the engagement ends?" — 90-day hyper-care, 30-day shadow hand-over with the client's chosen run team, full documentation pack, source code in the client's GitHub org from day one.
- **Linked depth:** every answer links to the underlying technology, methodology, or service page where deeper proof lives.
- **Last reviewed date** at the top of each answer; reviewed quarterly by Head of Delivery, Head of Legal, and Head of Risk.
- **The "Procurement Pack":** a single downloadable PDF that contains all 40 answers, plus the sample MSA / SoW / DPA, plus the contract-language clauses for IP and exit — designed to drop into a procurement file as-is.

## 11. Top 3 Objections
- **O1:** "Will this page give me a real number, or will I have to schedule a call to find out the cost?"
- **O2:** "Are your contract terms procurement-friendly, or will legal redline 60 % of the SoW?"
- **O3:** "If we end the engagement, does the model keep running, do we get the weights, and do we own the code?"
